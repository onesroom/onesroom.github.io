Rebecca's letter on beauty, truth and representations

发布于 2021-02-03 | 分类于 Gibberish | 10分钟 | 1481字数 
Dear lord, I confess.

In my heart there is not shame nor guilt,

in my eyes there is no aspiration but inanity,

in my face there shows nothing more only disguise,

through the ages I wandered aimlessly.

 

Under the city lights I cavort and whirl,

flowers and glasses all around me,

alone in the darkness I huddle up,

o dear child why is the whining?

Rebecca   8 ,2018

Dear Zoli,

Above is a piece of my 80%. I have been rejecting this kind of expression for months. I say, stop playing word games and movies inside your head. It's unhealthy' and you are covering up the reality. Imaginations were natural to me but I decided to exchange them for sobriety. I say, imaginations are beautiful. Beauty needs cover-ups and embellishment, but truths don't. This is one reason, I think truths over-weigh beauty. And a thing as it is is the truth. Then there are others.

As I watch back my history, I see how things developed. I came from the little village I told you. We were farmers, fishermen, lower-class workers and later some became businessmen, including my father. We have all virtues and ugliness of villagers but nothing exceeds. We, fathers and mothers never read or think beyond the practical. If you have seen My Brilliant Friend, you will understand better what I mean here. Elena wanted to escape where she grows up and learned to speak the language of a well-educated person, married a well-educated man at last. She got rid of the threat of the way her mother walks, the way fathers fight for food and wine, the way women shout at their children, the dirty floor and dirty jokes. Later she realized that it's all the same everywhere, only some people are better at disguising and cleaning their floor.

I was supposed to think like a villager but no, education and city life took this chance from me. But is education changing humans? Elena asks the same question. Reading Homer or Marx makes a better person? You learn to appreciate arts, to listen to classicals, to debate and wind up neat words, does this change anything about who you are? I have learned to think both in the way of my fathers and the educated group. I have found that this seemingly two different mindset ended up in the same direction of human sickness and mediocrity. And I, like Elena, not satisfied, try escaping, not only from the dirty floor but also from neat words. My solution is the poetical, the subtle, the existential, the imaginative, the lunatic, the chaotic, the destructive. These grew into my 80%. I took my life seriously into these games and searched ceaselessly for their rules. The problem is, it can never do this way. I've chosen to despise the living world and merge myself in a imaginative realm. This project itself would tear me apart because there is only one actual way of leaving the living world and abandoning the 20%, the cost of which I can not afford. This is the second reason.If I live the 20%, the 80% can't be genuine. If I dream the 80%, the 20% can't be genuine either. I can't be genuine to both.

The third is, I can't find anyone like me. If I try to reach them that way, they don't know how to respond. Lying on the knees of he who I like, I have to switch to his language; laughing with friends, I have to switch to their language. Playing my games with others is risky. Even a letter like this one is risky.

And another one. I became a slave of the imaginative world. I've actually mentioned this before. I want liberation.

I thus chose the only one possible way, back to the world of my fathers and my peers. It seems healthy, good. I say, finally, after blinding yourself into fantasies for such a time, you can feel the earth again and regain your nature, be as human as everyone else. Are you satisfied? After the first moments of liberation and feelings of triumph, I know I'm not satisfied, the 80% means more to me whatsoever. To the end, there is only one problem: if only I could be genuine in this world, this only world, even with my fantasies all along with me. All those stupid reasons should gone. But I can't think of a solution other than stop thinking right now. Actually, I wasn't going to discuss this topic but about the representation of oneself. They both involve similar subjects though. Words are representation, photos are representation, facial expressions are representation...... If one has to represent himself, how close is the representation to himself, how genuine could it be? Is it possible for people to truly understand each other by this representation?

If I live, and walk, and see, and feel, But silently, quietly, without representations of word, I can be genuine and free...like an animal...

Best wishes,

Rebecca

Dear Zoli,

Indeed, truth and beauty are not opposite or incompatible. These are concepts, then obscure images or flows in our head that we can't actually distinguish clearly. But a problem exits in my mind which can't be canceled by the connection of the two no matter how dialectically one can think. This problem is right about what genuine means to me.

What role does truth play in this? This truth is the way I can think, the mindset of the common world applied in me. Just by growing up in this world crowded with never-aesthetic feelings and perspectives I learn to think in the way that can't be beautiful to me. It's like some kind of pollution that you can't get away with. It's not about how things are, how the world rotates, but always about my own mind. I certainly can have fantasies and my own little cosmos running anywhere anytime. But at the same moment I can judge a person by his mere appearance the way my mother does, or fear the consequences of not obeying or smiling when you 'should be doing so. This thought and many many others are spontaneous and thus the way I am, the truth. If I avoid them because they are considered not beautiful, then I'm not genuine to myself and others. In other words, I have a picture of the kind of me that I like and accept, and beauty is only a metaphor for this. Truth is another metaphor for how I was raised up as a normal kid and continuously affected by things I resist while can't resist. And the resistance by means of representing differently and embellishing yourself with whatever it might be, causes dishonesty. This is worse than the not-so-beautiful truth.

When Adam and Eve were in Eden, they were like happy innocent animals. But they ate the not-supposed-to eat and began to feel naked, to gain self-awareness, to be god-like or, human-like. This is innocence lost. Wisdom, at the cost of innocence. Is it a good deal? As a kid I seldom reflect upon what I am but can go with the flow. Then we know the right, the wrong, the should, the should-not......We are no longer beasts but are we healthier? Thinking and consciousness are like adversaries of instincts and nature. We can't go back to Eden though. We can't pretend that we don't know. We can't just erase the brain statistics and leave behind our past. The least of what we can do now is to be genuine, to represent what we really are at this very moment. And we will continue learning and approaching the light we pursue until bit by bit our future self is shaped by a past of what we consider to be beautiful now.

[Extra Ending，
Sometimes I thought of rotting in this place. My home town this village. Stay here and teach little kids. Group a little chorus and renovate this old house of my family's. Keep untouched from anyone in my past and forget about city language. Lead the simplest life and rot here with no connection to outer world. If possible, no touch from anyone outside of the house and rot in the dark alone. Disappear. Delete any single trace of the evidence that I once lived here or there. Vaporize. ]

My hesitation, my contradiction, my hope, my pessimism and all, can you see? There can be no truth of me or you without grey sides. I try to represent as close to me as possible. Maybe soon you will know me better than myself, teacher.]

Sending virtual hug,
Rebecca
